📜 What is Ram Shalaka Prashnavali?

Ram Shalaka is a 15x15 square grid—a chart where each cell contains a letter that is part of a chaupai (couplet) from the Ramcharitmanas, authored by Goswami Tulsidas. It is mentioned in various other Hindu scriptures as well.

In one episode from the Ramcharitmanas, a few saints doubted the power of the Ramayan. They placed it beneath the Vedas in the evening—but to their surprise, the next morning, the Ramcharitmanas was found above all the other scriptures. This sacred grid is sometimes referred to as a Prashnavali.

📊 The Ram Shalaka Prashnavali Chart: Explained

The Ram Shalaka Prashnavali chart is a unique table containing individual characters (aksharas) from nine specific chaupais of the Ramcharitmanas. Each box represents a letter that belongs to one of these chaupais. When selected, it leads you to one of the nine divine answers, helping devotees gain spiritual clarity and make informed decisions.

🧠 Understanding the Ramcharitmanas

Goswami Tulsidas ji retold the Ramcharitmanas, an epic poem narrating the story of Lord Rama in the Awadhi language.

About Goswami Tulsidas

Tulsidas (1532–1623) was a 16th-century Indian poet, saint, and ardent devotee of Lord Rama, born in Rajapur, Uttar Pradesh, India. It is one of the longest poems in the world, containing over 24,000 shlokas (verses).

The Ramcharitmanas is not just a literary work but a sacred scripture, recited and revered in homes, temples, and festivals across India even today.

The Ramayana is divided into seven Kandas (sections):

Bala Kanda

Describes Rama's birth, childhood, and early life in Ayodhya.

Ayodhya Kanda

Covers Rama's exile, Dasharatha's dilemma, and departure.

Aranya Kanda

Narrates life in the forest and Sita's abduction by Ravana.

Kishkindha Kanda

Details Rama's alliance with Sugriva, the death of Bali, meeting Hanuman ji, and the search for Ma Sita.

Sundara Kanda

Focuses on Hanuman's journey to Lanka, finding Sita, and his heroic deeds.

Lanka Kanda

Depicts the battle between Rama and Ravana, Sita's rescue, and Rama's victory.

Uttara Kanda

Chronicles Rama's return to Ayodhya, his rule, Sita's exile, and their final life events.
